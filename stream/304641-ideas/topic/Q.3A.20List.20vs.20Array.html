Qqwy / Marten (Jul 01 2022 at 20:45):

Thinking a little more about this:

  • Especially because Roc is in the intersection of different communities, it feels extra important to not break the 'Principle of Least Astonishment'.
  • I've only ever seen people introduce Roc List as "List is actually a [dynamic] array", which as @Brian Carroll already noted might indicate that it is the clearer name.
  • Even if people were to confuse a datatype named Array to be a 'static' array at first, that is not a problem: Static arrays and dynamic arrays have virtually identical performance and memory characteristics; the main difference is that dynamic arrays permit a small amount of extra operations (growing/shrinking).
  • In other words: Nothing is lost if you are not aware from the get-go that Array is dynamic; and if you do encounter Array.append / Array.slice / Array.dropLast at some point, the change to your mental model will be tiny.
