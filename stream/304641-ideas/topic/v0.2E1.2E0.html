Brendan Hansknecht (Dec 20 2024 at 05:06):

What do we think about the major platform changes:

  1. Explicitly passing in allocators and effects as a struct into roc when calling it.
  2. Exposing roc type to the host via cffi to make glue manageable and more robust
  3. Fixing our mix of cffi issues such that platforms hit less edge cases.

These will be major platform changes. Some breaking. And fixing stuff to make glue functional will be important when we want more platforms. (Especially platforms not managed by luke)

Oh, and I guess if we have any plans for atomics and sharing across threads (but I definitely think that can be post 0.1.0)

Richard Feldman (Dec 20 2024 at 05:09):

great questions! I think all of those wouldn't necessarily be 0.1.0 blockers, unless they turned out to be prerequisites for something else

Richard Feldman (Dec 20 2024 at 05:10):

e.g. it might turn out that for Rust platforms like basic-cli and basic-webserver to work on Windows (which they currently don't, as far as I know) we need to switch to one-way linking, which would be blocked on the allocators change

Richard Feldman (Dec 20 2024 at 05:11):

Richard Feldman (Dec 20 2024 at 05:11):

Richard Feldman (Dec 20 2024 at 05:12):

Richard Feldman (Dec 20 2024 at 05:22):

to me, the theme of 0.1.0 is:

  • Roc is now reliable enough (after lambda set fixes, Windows fixes, platform fixes) that you can try it out and not worry that the compiler is going to crash all the time. You may still encounter bugs (it's 0.1, not 1.0, etc.) but it's not like it used to be back in the days when I would have huge disclaimers for beginners every time I talked about Roc in public. :big_smile:
  • From a design perspective, Roc is now about as friendly to beginners coming from non-FP backgrounds as we anticipate it ever being. So that's purity inference, var, for, and static dispatch. Between this and the previous point, we should get a strong signal from the beginners coming in regarding how easy Roc has become to get into, without the noise of the main blocker being "I keep hitting compiler crashes." :big_smile: Presumably there will still be room for improvement in varioous ways, but this should give us a good sense of the impact the new things have had on the beginner experience!
  • Roc is now at a point where you can actually build nontrivial applications (e.g. a realworld backend would be sweet) using things that in 2024 have only been in design documents and discussions, so we can now actually try out the new styles that are available and see how they feel to use in practice!
