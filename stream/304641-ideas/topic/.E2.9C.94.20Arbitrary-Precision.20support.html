What were the thoughts of doing what Lean 4 does and natively supporting arbitrary-precision.

  • "the internal representation of Nat is optimized. Small natural numbers (i.e., < 2^63 in a 64-bit machine) are represented by a single machine word. Big numbers are implemented using GMP numbers."

Richard Feldman (Jul 16 2023 at 19:33):

we've talked about it before - basically my thinking is:

  • I128 lets you represent numbers in the undecillions
  • usually when people talk about something being "multiple orders of magnitude faster" it's hyperbole for emphasis, but in this case, I128 is literally multiple orders of magnitude faster than arbitrary-sized integers, because they can live entirely in CPU registers instead of requiring heap allocations or chasing pointers
  • what are the specific use cases where undecillions are insufficient, and we need something bigger, and are those specific use cases common enough to justify inclusion in the standard library?
