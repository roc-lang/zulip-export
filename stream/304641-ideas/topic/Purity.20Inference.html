Alexander Pyattaev (Aug 28 2024 at 18:27):

Overall I suggest you do either or. Both approaches have merits:

  • Tasks as they are now are appealing to people coming from Rust, as Task is essentially same as impl Future, and that makes sense. Syntax around tasks is not the best, sure, but it "makes sense"
  • The proposed Result! thing is by far more ergonomic, but in this case the Task interface should not be exposed to users of the language at all, and fully deprecated. There is no reason to keep both interfaces around, all it will do is cause confusion.

As pointed out above, ! is probably not the best symbol for this due to its semantic meaning in sentences, and also since it is commonly used as a "not" operator in many languages (sadly, not something roc does, but one can dream). Maybe something like $ or @ could be considered instead? And yes, in rust ! is also kind of inappropriate at macro call sites as it can be mistaken for a "not" operator which uses the same symbol. It would also separate it more from the ? operator.

some notes:

  • regarding "it is commonly used as a 'not' operator in many languages (sadly, not something roc does, but one can dream)" - your dream has retroactively come true! !foo in Roc already means "not foo" and has for several years - I think maybe even since the first version of the parser, although it's been long enough that I don't remember anymore! :big_smile:
  • Rust having both ! as a prefix (for "not") and as a suffix (for macros) and it being totally fine in practice was part of the reason I figured it would be fine to have ! as both a prefix and a suffix at the expression level in Roc (which is what we have today), but worth noting that this proposal would go back to having only prefix ! (meaning "not" as usual) at the expression level
  • in the proposal, the ! suffix would mean "effectfulness" only in type annotations, and in type annotations there has never been a ! prefix
