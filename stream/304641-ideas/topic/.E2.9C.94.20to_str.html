Richard Feldman (Dec 30 2025 at 19:11):

I should probably write about this somewhere, but philosophically the idea behind this design is:

  • when you just want to debug print something or write it to a log (so, not to be displayed to end users), that should be super easy - Str.inspect accepts any type and does that, without anyone needing to do anything
  • if you're making a new type and want to customize how it looks with Str.inspect, there's a method you can opt into defining to do that
  • there's no conflation of "display to end user" and "display for programmer internal use" - which there is with to_str

Richard Feldman (Dec 30 2025 at 19:12):

which is how you get things like end users seeing null or undefined rendered to them :sweat_smile:

Richard Feldman (Dec 30 2025 at 19:13):

Try shouldn't have a "display to end users" method, but it should have a "turn into a string for debugging/logging purposes"

Richard Feldman (Dec 30 2025 at 19:14):

and if it had to_str, it would be easy for end users to accidentally see Ok displayed to them
