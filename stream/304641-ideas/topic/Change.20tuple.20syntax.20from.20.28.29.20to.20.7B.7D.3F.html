Topic: Change tuple syntax from () to {}?

Sky Rose (Dec 17 2024 at 14:11):

What if we used curly braces for tuples instead of parens?

old: ("abc", 123)
new: {"abc", 123}

Pros:

  • Tuples are a lot like records without keys / with index keys.
  • Avoids ambiguity with parens for grouping (#contributing > Ambigous syntax for tuples and functions )
  • Makes syntax for 1-tuples reasonable (but we still don't necessarily want them) (#ideas > 1-tuples )

Cons:

  • Cost of switching
  • A less familiar syntax for most languages (though not unprecedented. Elixir at least uses {} for tuples)

There would be a couple new ambiguities with records, but these are minor compared to ambiguity with parens for grouping:

  • Need a new set of error messages if you have a {} where some have keys and some don't.
  • {} could be an empty tuple or an empty record, but I don't think it matters.

Anthony Bullard (Dec 17 2024 at 14:46):

Yeah, you can't do this and ALSO have field punning in records. And the advantages of punning outweight any sort of benefit one could imagine from changing the tuple syntax. And the only other brace pair that is unused in the language is <> and I don't think anyone wants to use <"abc", 123>. The other option is to do what Elixir does and use a sigil for differentiating between records and tuples:

Tuple: {"abc", 123}
Struct: %{hello, world} or #{hello, world}

I think the sigil approach makes tuples more ergonomic than records, which is not what I think we want to do

So to summarize, options I've seen are:

  • Leave as (), navigate the ambiguities and conflicts with other uses of parens.
  • {}, have some way to disambiguate with punned record field names, like requiring {0: in ambiguous cases.
  • sigil before {}. #{}, %{}, ~{}, |{}, {| |}or something like that.
    • My favorite is #{}, which I read as "A record, but with numbers as keys, and # is the number sign".
  • sigil before (). %(), ~(), :(), or something like that.
    • #() is what Gleam uses.
  • <>, the fourth bracket type.
  • Remove tuples from the language Rejected by #ideas > ✔ Do we need tuples?
